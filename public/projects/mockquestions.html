MockQuestions

Your shortcut to success in interviews

Get professional and relevant interview answers for your dream career. Find the right position, learn about common questions in the workplace, and prepare with confidence for your next interview.

Year
2023
Tools
Figma
Industry
Career Development, Job Preparation, HR Tech, Recruitment Support
Project Overview:
1

This project is a web and mobile based platform designed to help job seekers prepare for job interviews. Users can search for career positions by industry, filter by company, and access a list of interview questions categorized by role type or profession. The system also provides sample answers and guidance from experts to help users improve their chances of success in the job selection process.

🚨 Problem

1. Candidates struggle to find interview questions relevant to the positions they are applying for.
2. Information about the interview process at various companies is scattered and unstructured.
3. Candidates lack confidence because they don't know how to answer questions correctly.

💡 Solution
1

1. Provides a database of interview questions based on specific professions and roles.
2. Offers a career search feature with company filters for more specific results.
3. Displays professional sample answers and tips from HR experts to help users respond accurately.
4. Easy and fast navigation access

💻 My Role and Responsibility
2

As a UX/UI Designer on this project, I was responsible for designing the user flow based on two main scenarios: career search and interview question list exploration.

I designed an intuitive, responsive, and user friendly interface, ensuring an optimal user experience across various devices. Throughout the process, I created wireframes, high fidelity mockups, and interactive prototypes in Figma to illustrate efficient search flows and information access.

Additionally, I organized the information structure systematically to make it easy for users to navigate features such as company filters and professional categories. I applied principles of visual hierarchy and usability to help users stay focused on their primary goals: finding the right position and preparing well for job interviews.

Process

The design process for this project was divided into four key phases to ensure a user centered and results experience:

Discovery

At this step, I analyzed competitors to understand existing approaches in the market. In addition, I identified pain points that users often experience in the job search and interview preparation process. A deep understanding of user needs and target audiences is an important foundation before entering the design phase.

Ideation

In this step, I began to develop user flows and user journeys to illustrate how users would interact with the product as a whole. The goal was to ensure that each step felt logical, efficient, and aligned with users' needs in achieving their objectives.

Design Process

This stage includes developing a design system to maintain visual and functional consistency across all interface elements. Based on the defined structure and flow, I designed a responsive and user friendly final design, focusing on ease of navigation and information readability.

Usability Testing

Once the final design is complete, I create scenarios and tasks to test the usability and effectiveness of the interface. This testing helps identify friction points and areas that need improvement to ensure the user experience is truly optimal and aligned with the platform's objectives.

Discovery
3
Discover and Understanding

Before moving on to the design, it is important to understand the context of the problem, user behavior, and how similar solutions have been implemented in the industry. This step helps ensure that the design is relevant and truly addresses user needs.

Competitor Analysis

To gain deeper insights into existing approaches, I conducted an analysis of several platforms that offer career search and job interview preparation features. From this analysis, I compared the main features, strengths, and weaknesses to identify opportunities for improvement in the solution I designed

Pain Points

1. Interview question databases are often too general or not tailored to specific roles.
2. Many platforms separate job search from interview preparation, forcing users to switch between tools.
3. Interfaces are sometimes outdated or unintuitive for first time users.
4. Users lack confidence due to absence of structured, step by step interview preparation.

User & Audience

1. Recent graduates seeking their first job and needing structured interview preparation.
2. Job switchers looking for insights into new industries or companies.
3. Candidates aiming to understand specific expectations from different roles.
4. Users who prefer simple, task focused platforms without clutter.
5. Individuals who want quick access to frequently asked interview questions filtered by role or company.

Ideation
4

After understanding user needs and competitor gaps, the ideation stage focuses on designing the overall user flow and experience. Here, I put together user flows and user journeys to make sure users can reach their goals smoothly, efficiently, and intuitively.

User Flow

User flows help map the steps users take when using the product, from the initial page to reaching a specific goal. With these flows, I ensure that every interaction is logical and minimizes obstacles.

User Story: As a user, I want to easily access job listings so that I can quickly find relevant opportunities.

User Story: As a user, I want to view interview questions and answers for specific roles so that I can prepare more effectively for upcoming interviews.

User Journey

User journey maps are used to understand the user experience in a more emotional and contextual way. By mapping each step, I can identify opportunities for improvement and create a better experience from the user's perspective.

Scenario: I want to see which companies are hiring for a Product Manager position.

Expectation: To view relevant job postings through a simple and easy to follow flow.

Design Process
5

To maintain visual consistency and efficiency in the design process, I created a design system that includes key UI components such as buttons, cards, color systems, and typography. This design system is designed to be easy to reuse and support responsive interface development, especially for mobile and desktop needs.

Design System
Final Design
Homepage

The intro section of the homepage acts as a hero section that directly conveys the platform's main value: providing effective interview answers and coaching. With a strong headline, professional illustrations, and a prominent search bar, this section encourages users to immediately search for interview content that suits their needs.

Profession Coverage & More Interview Questions

This section introduces the wide range of professions available on the platform, displayed through category cards such as Nursing, Teacher, Accounting, and Marketer. Below that, there are previews of interview questions and sample answers in an interactive display. This provides a direct overview of the content offered and encourages users to explore more through a clear call to action button.

Interview Experts

This section features a list of experts who contributed to the compilation of interview answers. Displayed in the form of profile cards complete with names and job titles, this section builds trust by showing that the content comes from experienced professionals who understand the recruitment process firsthand.

Our Directory

The directory section makes it easy for users to search for interview questions based on relevant filters such as topic, career, category, and educational institution. Enhanced with example categories like “Accomplishment” and “Adaptability,” users can see the number of questions available, making the exploration experience more structured and personalized.

Testimonial

The testimonial section presents direct reviews from users who have experienced the benefits of the platform. With a combination of expressive visuals and short quotes, this section serves as social validation to increase new users' trust in the credibility and quality of the service.

Our Statistics

The statistics section shows the platform's achievements quantitatively, such as the number of projects completed, the number of satisfied clients, and the number of answers provided. This information is presented visually to reinforce the platform's professional and trustworthy image.

Footer

The footer provides additional navigation information such as About, Privacy Policy, and Sitemap. This section neatly concludes the page while offering quick access to important information regarding the platform's structure, legal aspects, and usage policies.

Pop up Search Bar

This page is a pop up navigation that appears after users click on the search bar on the homepage. The main purpose of this pop up is to facilitate quick and structured exploration of interview content. At the top, there is a prominent search bar to help users find specific interview questions. Below that, the content is grouped into four categories: Careers, Companies, Topics, and Featured Articles. Each category presents a comprehensive list, such as popular professions (e.g., Nursing or Project Manager), major companies (e.g., Google and Amazon), and specific interview topics (Leadership, Communication, etc.). On the right side, the Featured Articles and Membership sections provide access to articles and login or coaching features. This pop up allows users to find content quickly without having to switch pages, improving overall navigation efficiency.

Career Page

This page is the result of navigation after the user clicks on the Career category from the search pop up. The main focus of this display is to present various types of interview questions based on topics in the selected career context. On the left side, there is an Interview Filter panel that allows users to narrow down results based on additional categories such as Companies, Schools, Topics, and Industries. In the main section of the page, each card displays question topics such as Accomplishment, Adaptability, and Customer Service, complete with the contributor's name and a “Start Interview” button to immediately begin the question and answer session. This page provides a structured, flexible, and easy to navigate content exploration experience tailored to the user's needs.

Filter by Company

This page is displayed after the user selects the Companies category from the filter panel on the previous page. Here, users are presented with a collection of popular Featured Companies such as Amazon, Google, Facebook, and Pfizer. Each company has a list of related job positions, such as Software Engineer, Project Manager, or Customer Service Specialist, complete with an icon to start the interview. This display provides quick access for users targeting job interviews at specific companies, accelerating the preparation process through a more focused and relevant approach.

Interview Question and Answers

This Q&A interview page presents a list of questions and answers relevant to the specific role selected by the user, in this example a position in Marketing. The main section of the page displays one open question complete with answers from experts and other users, followed by a list of other questions that can be accessed by clicking on the dropdown menu. Below that are “Related Career Interviews,” which show other roles within the same field, and “Helpful Company Interviews,” which provide a list of companies with interview questions related to that role. These components make the page feel comprehensive, making it easy for users to explore the information they need in one integrated page.

Side navigation is an additional feature that enriches the user experience by providing quick access to the page's content structure. When inactive, the side nav is hidden to keep the layout clean and provide more reading space. However, when active, the side nav appears on the left side of the page and transforms into a Table of Contents-like feature covering sections such as “General Questions,” “Behavioral Questions,” or other subcategories depending on the role being discussed. This allows users to directly access the sections they are interested in without needing to scroll extensively, which is highly useful when users want to prepare for interviews more efficiently and effectively.

Usability Testing

To evaluate the overall usability, effectiveness, and user satisfaction of the job interview preparation platform, I conducted a series of moderated usability testing sessions with participants who represented typical job seekers. Each session was designed around realistic, goal oriented scenarios that reflected common tasks users would perform when preparing for job interviews using the platform.

The primary objective of this usability testing was to observe how easily users could navigate the platform’s features such as searching for career positions by industry, filtering companies, and accessing categorized interview questions. I also aimed to assess whether the sample answers and expert guidance provided clear, actionable support, and if the overall user interface helped users feel confident and prepared for their job interviews.

Participants were given the following scenario to complete:
1. As a job seeker, you want to see what positions are currently available. How do you search for them?
2. You want to know what role you need, like a Marketing. How do you find this information?
3. You have a dream to work at a certain company, like a Amazon. How do you find this information?
4. You also want to learn what questions and answers will be asked during the interview. What can you do?
5. You want to know the interview questions and answers specific to the Marketing position. What do you do?

These tasks were designed to evaluate the intuitiveness of the content organization, the effectiveness of filtering options, and the clarity of guidance materials. By analyzing user interactions, feedback, and task completion rates, I gathered critical insights that informed design improvements. The findings ensured that the platform meets job seekers’ needs by enhancing accessibility, simplifying information discovery, and boosting user confidence throughout their interview preparation journey.

Reflection

If I had additional time, I would:
1. Continued usability testing with the created scenarios.
2. Conduct an in depth evaluation of visual consistency and responsive design.
3. Iterate if feedback is received during usability testing.
