Project Overview:
1

Futuri Content Cloud is designed to address the major challenges faced by content creators, brands, and social media teams in consistently building an audience. With AI-based technology, this platform not only suggests what to post and when the best time to post is, but also provides in-depth analytics to measure impact, engagement, and audience growth. For anyone serious about building influence in the digital age, this is a strategic tool that accelerates the achievement of their content goals.

Process

The design process for this project was divided into four key phases to ensure a user centered and results experience:

Discovery

Conducting competitor analysis, digital content trend research, and user interviews to gather insights and understand the key needs and challenges faced by content creators.

Ideation

Brainstorming solutions based on discovery findings. Developing user flows, identifying core features, and prioritizing user needs in the form of initial wireframes.

Design Process

Developing high fidelity UI based on a consistent design system. Creating interactive prototypes to test flow and visual consistency, while optimizing readability and information hierarchy.

Usability Testing

Developing relevant testing tasks and scenarios, such as finding the best time to post content, checking audience insights from a piece of content, and accessing content performance results from various platforms.

Competitor Analysis

To understand Futuri Content Cloud's position in the market, I conducted a competitor analysis of several similar platforms that have similar functions, which is to support content strategies based on data and AI. This analysis covers key features, strengths, weaknesses, and the design approaches they employ. From this case, I found that many platforms excel in one area but lack integration in combining audience insights, content strategies, and collaboration tools. This finding presents an opportunity for Futuri to position itself as a more cohesive end to end solution.

Pain Points

From analyzing various content and social media platforms, we found some common pain points that users experience:
1. Many platforms only focus on scheduling, without supporting the ideation process or long term content strategy.
2. The interface is complicated and confusing for beginners, making it hard to onboard new users.
3. Insights and analytics are shallow, not helpful enough for strategy based decision making.
4. High subscription costs, especially on platforms with comprehensive features, making access difficult for independent creators or small businesses.
5. Lack of seamless integration between content planning, audience analytics, and team collaboration processes.
6. Many platforms feel too corporate or rigid, with user interfaces and experiences that are less engaging for the creative industry.

User & Audience

Based on usage patterns and competitor product positioning, several main user types can be identified:
1. Individual content creators who need simple and fast tools to manage their content.
2. Social media managers who require efficiency in scheduling and reporting content performance.
3. Small business owners who produce their own content and want audience insights without needing a large team.
4. Marketing teams at agencies or startups that require a collaborative platform with editorial control, insights, and flexible workflows.
5. Communication teams at large companies that need in depth analytics solutions, approval systems, and multi platform integration, automation

Usability Testing

To evaluate the overall effectiveness, usability, and user satisfaction of the Futuri Content Cloud platform, I conducted a series of moderated usability testing sessions with participants representing potential users of the platform. These sessions were carefully structured to simulate real world usage, using realistic scenarios combined with goal oriented tasks that reflect a new user‚Äôs journey when discovering and exploring Futuri Content Cloud.

The main goal of this usability testing was to observe how users interact with key features, how easily they can navigate the interface, and whether the design choices, such as content hierarchy, button placement, and terminology successfully guide users toward achieving their goals. Furthermore, I aimed to identify any usability issues or pain points that could hinder the overall user experience and determine whether the platform effectively aligns with user needs, expectations, and motivations.

By analyzing participant behavior, feedback, and performance in completing tasks, I was able to gather valuable insights to inform potential design improvements. These findings also helped validate whether the current product direction supports users through every stage of their content creation journey from discovery and exploration to engagement and confidence.

Participants were given the following scenario to complete:
1. You are interested in becoming a content creator but are still unsure of what to do, and you have heard that Futuri Content Cloud can solve your problems. Now you want to know more about Futuri Content Cloud, so what do you do?
2. You see that Futuri Content Cloud provides a community for sharing information related to content creators, and you want to join. What can you do?
3. You understand Futuri Content Cloud, but you still want to explore its features. How do you do that?
4. Right now, you're facing challenges and feeling confused. What should you do?
5. You also want to know the latest news about Futuri Content Cloud to build your confidence in using this platform. What should you do?
You are 100% confident in using Futuri Content Cloud. What can you do?
